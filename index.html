DSDL is a 5-7 days workshop conducted by a government agency where the service beneficiaries, service providers and ICT experts work together to analyze manual services, design digital services and prepare implementation plan for the digital transformation of the agency.

About Us

In 2008, the Honorable Prime Minister of Bangladesh, Sheikh Hasina declared "Vision 2021: Digital Bangladesh". Inspired by the vision, ministries and govt. organizations took various initiatives for public service digitization and e-Gov implementation, most of which were silo, ad-hoc basis, scattered and developed without any common standard approach or precise plan. Therefore, a2i, Prime Minister’s Office innovated a customized but effective design and planning model for service digitalization and capacity enhancement which is widely known as Digital Service Design Lab (DSDL). This model is very popular in Bangladesh Govt. for its uniqueness and impacts. For public service digitalization, this is a synergistic planning approach i.e. service recipient, service provider, decision makers and technology experts plan together sharing their knowledge and experiences. It has step-by-step methodical approach but A-Z i.e. study, diagnosis, analysis, design & plan of digitalization which is basically hands on work for consecutive 7 days to develop e-Government Masterplan. This results extremely high ownership of Govt. officials generating maximum output in minimum time enhancing digitalization capacity significantly.
